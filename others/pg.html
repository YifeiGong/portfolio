The Problem

My group and I worked with P&G in our Human-Centered Design Studio: Product Design Course to improve Pampers Diapers Products Using Experience by Sustainable Diaper Design to reduce plastic and make more environmental impacts.




The Take-Aways

Through this Studio Course, I learned and applied Human-Centered Design Approach by In-Home Visit and Central Site Visit combined with synthesis and iteration week by week. I built up my product design sense by this consumer packaged goods project which has very different product design process with my past experience and considered more about user’s tensions and perspectives.
Transiting from Data-Driven Design back to Human-Centered Design again is my most challenge part. But the most exciting part was also the user research part. Talking with the real users and getting into their real living setting to do research made me feel as a product designer, I am more intimate with users, and more immersive of this design topic I’ve never considered before.
In this course, I also reinforced my product design process from user research and secondary research, to synthesis, and to define the problem and iterate solution by sketching, prototyping and testing.

The Research

By following the requirements and guidance from mentors from Procter and Gamble, we focused on our target users and conducted our research plan and discussion guide. Under the real home setting and changing context, we started with user’s tensions for the currently using process and experience, even more emotion brought by the product details.




The Ideation and Iteration

According to our insights and design requirement got from the research stage, we delivered some wide range of product design ideas to solve the problem. And we chosed to developed some selected idea which separately focuses on creativity, simplicity, and hygiene.

After that, we made and presented our minimum viable prototypes to users in Central Site Visit one. By collecting many feedbacks from users and mentor, we adjusted our design direction after midterm. We developed some stronger idea, discarded some not available idea and created some new ideas to test in our CSV 2.

After CSV2, we validated some design features and simplified some design back to the initial one. Based on this finding, we conducted our final results.




The Pitch

Our team designed an innovative system including product design and service for baby’s changing size to solve the current sustainable diaper environmental impact ambiguity and high access threshold problem to give mothers another option when they are making diapering decision.
